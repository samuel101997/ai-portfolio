NOVATEL MOBILE - CUSTOMER ESCALATION PROCEDURES
Version 1.5 | Effective: January 1, 2026
Internal Document - Customer Support Reference

====================================================================
SECTION 1: ESCALATION OVERVIEW
====================================================================

1.1 PURPOSE
This document defines when and how customer support agents should 
escalate customer issues beyond their own authority. Proper escalation 
ensures customers receive timely resolution while maintaining consistent 
service standards. Unnecessary escalation creates bottlenecks and delays 
for all customers.

1.2 ESCALATION PRINCIPLES
- Always attempt resolution at your level first
- Document all troubleshooting steps taken before escalating
- Never promise a specific outcome that is beyond your authority
- Never tell a customer "there's nothing we can do" — there is always 
  an escalation path
- Warm transfers are ALWAYS preferred over cold transfers (stay on the 
  line to brief the next agent)
- If a customer asks for a supervisor, acknowledge their frustration 
  first, attempt resolution, and if they insist, honor the request

SECTION 2: ESCALATION TIERS
====================================================================

2.1 TIER 1 - FRONTLINE SUPPORT (You)
Authority Level:
- Troubleshoot all standard issues (network, billing, device setup)
- Apply bill credits up to $50 per interaction
- Waive one-time fees up to $35 (e.g., SIM card fee, reconnection fee)
- Waive late payment fee once per 12-month period
- Process plan changes, add-ons, and feature modifications
- Process SIM replacements and eSIM activations
- Reset voicemail PINs and account PINs
- Enable/disable account features (roaming, hotspot, etc.)
- Apply authorized promotional offers
- Apply one retention offer (R1-R5) when customer states intent to cancel

Response Time Commitment: Resolve or escalate within 15 minutes.

2.2 TIER 2 - SENIOR SUPPORT
When to Escalate to Tier 2:
- Technical issues not resolved by standard troubleshooting
- Bill credits needed above $50 but below $200
- Complex billing disputes requiring account audit
- Device financing modifications or disputes
- Coverage complaints requiring network investigation
- Repeated issues (customer has called 3+ times for same problem)
- Data usage disputes

How to Escalate:
- Use the internal transfer tool, select "Tier 2 Senior Support"
- Include: Customer name, account number, summary of issue, all 
  troubleshooting steps taken
- Warm transfer: Stay on the line until Tier 2 agent picks up, 
  brief them on the situation, then disconnect

Tier 2 Authority:
- Bill credits up to $200
- Waive fees up to $100
- Override plan restrictions with justification
- Open network investigation tickets (Priority: High)
- Arrange courtesy device replacements
- Extend promotional pricing up to 6 months

Response Time: Pick up transferred call within 3 minutes. If queue 
exceeds 3 minutes, offer callback within 2 hours.

2.3 TIER 3 - SPECIALIST TEAMS
NovaTel has several specialist teams for specific issue types:

RETENTION TEAM
When to Escalate:
- Customer has explicitly stated intent to cancel AND
- You have offered one authorized retention offer (R1-R5) AND
- Customer declined the offer and still wishes to cancel
How to Reach: Transfer code RT-100
Authority: Up to 50% plan discount for 12 months, device credits up 
to $500, custom plan creation, port-out save offers

FRAUD & SECURITY TEAM
When to Escalate:
- Customer reports unauthorized account access
- Customer reports unauthorized SIM swap
- Customer reports unauthorized charges they did not make
- Suspected identity theft or account takeover
- Multiple failed verification attempts with suspicious circumstances
How to Reach: Transfer code FS-200
Authority: Immediate account freeze, SIM lock, fraud investigation 
initiation, full charge reversal pending investigation
IMPORTANT: For suspected fraud, do NOT make any account changes 
yourself. Transfer to Fraud team immediately.
Response Time: Must answer within 60 seconds (priority queue).

DATA USAGE REVIEW TEAM
When to Escalate:
- Customer disputes data usage shown on their account
- Customer claims they were throttled incorrectly
- Unusual data usage patterns (e.g., 40 GB used in one day)
How to Reach: Transfer code DU-300
Authority: Data usage audit, session-level data review, plan 
adjustments based on findings, credits for confirmed billing errors

NETWORK OPERATIONS CENTER (NOC)
When to Escalate:
- 911 service failure (MANDATORY - Priority 1)
- Widespread outage affecting multiple customers
- Persistent coverage issue after network ticket has been open 7+ days
- Customer is a Priority Business Account with SLA
How to Reach: Extension 9911 (for 911 failures), Transfer code NC-400 
(for other network issues)
Authority: Dispatch field technicians, tower maintenance scheduling, 
temporary cell site deployment

DEVICE SUPPORT TEAM
When to Escalate:
- Hardware defect assessment
- Warranty claim processing
- Complex device compatibility issues
- Trade-in valuation disputes
How to Reach: Transfer code DS-500
Authority: Warranty replacements, trade-in value overrides, device 
exchange authorization

2.4 TIER 4 - MANAGEMENT ESCALATION
When to Escalate:
- Customer has asked for a manager/supervisor by name
- Issue requires bill credit above $200
- Legal threats from the customer
- Media or social media threats ("I'm going to post this online")
- Regulatory complaint (CCTS complaint filed)
- Any situation where you believe exceptional authority is needed

How to Escalate:
- Contact your Team Lead directly via internal chat
- If Team Lead is unavailable, use Transfer code TL-600
- For CCTS complaints specifically, use Transfer code CC-700

Management Authority:
- Bill credits up to $1,000
- Full account restructuring
- Service recovery packages
- Direct correspondence with customer
- Coordination with Legal team if needed

Response Time: Manager callback within 1 business hour.

- Tier 2 to Specialist: Answered within 5 minutes
- Management callback: Within 1 business hour
- Network trouble ticket update: Every 48 hours until resolved
- Fraud case resolution: Within 10 business days

SECTION 4: COMPLAINT HANDLING PROCESS
====================================================================

4.1 STANDARD COMPLAINT PROCESS
Step 1: LISTEN - Let the customer explain the full issue without 
        interruption. Take notes.
Step 2: ACKNOWLEDGE - Express empathy. Use phrases like:
        "I understand how frustrating this must be."
        "I can see why this is concerning."
        Do NOT say: "I understand" without context, "That's our policy," 
        or "There's nothing I can do."
Step 3: INVESTIGATE - Review the account, check previous interactions, 
        identify the root cause.
Step 4: RESOLVE - Offer a solution within your authority. If the issue 
        requires escalation, explain what will happen next.
Step 5: CONFIRM - Ensure the customer agrees with the resolution. 
        Summarize what was done and any follow-up actions.
Step 6: DOCUMENT - Log the full interaction in the CRM including: 
        issue category, root cause, resolution applied, and any 
        credits issued.

4.2 HANDLING ANGRY OR ABUSIVE CUSTOMERS
- Remain calm and professional at all times
- Do not take personal offense at venting or frustration
- If a customer uses profanity directed AT you (personal attacks), 
  you may say: "I want to help resolve this for you, but I need our 
  conversation to remain respectful. I'm here to find a solution."
- If personal attacks continue after one warning, you may say: 
  "I'm going to transfer you to a supervisor who may be better able 
  to assist." Transfer to Team Lead (TL-600).
- NEVER hang up on a customer. Always transfer.
- Document abusive interactions in the CRM with the tag "Conduct Issue"

4.3 CCTS COMPLAINTS
The Commission for Complaints for Telecom-television Services (CCTS) 
is Canada's independent telecommunications complaint body.

When a customer mentions CCTS:
1) Inform the customer that NovaTel takes all complaints seriously and 
   you'd like the opportunity to resolve it first
2) If the customer insists on filing with CCTS, provide:
   - CCTS Website: www.ccts-cprst.ca
   - CCTS Phone: 1-888-221-1687
   - CCTS accepts complaints about: billing, contracts, service 
     delivery, and credit management
3) If a CCTS complaint has ALREADY been filed (customer has a CCTS 
   case number), escalate immediately to Transfer code CC-700
4) Do NOT attempt to resolve a formally filed CCTS complaint yourself
5) CCTS complaints have a regulatory 30-day response deadline

SECTION 5: PRIORITY CUSTOMER HANDLING
====================================================================

5.1 PRIORITY BUSINESS ACCOUNTS
Customers flagged as "Business Priority" in the CRM have enhanced SLAs:
- Dedicated support line: 1-800-NOVA-BIZ (1-800-668-2249)
- Maximum hold time: 2 minutes
- Issue resolution target: Same business day
- Assigned Account Manager (check CRM for manager name and contact)
- If Account Manager is unavailable, treat as Tier 2 escalation

5.2 ACCESSIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
NovaTel is committed to accessible service:
- TTY/TDD support available: 1-800-NOVA-TTY (1-800-668-2889)
- Video relay service supported
- Large-print bills available on request (no charge)
- Braille bills available on request (no charge, allow 10 business 
  days for processing)
- Alternative format contracts available on request
- Agents must allow additional time for customers using accessibility 
  services — do NOT rush these interactions

5.3 VULNERABLE CUSTOMER POLICY
If you identify that a customer may be in a vulnerable situation 
(elderly, disability, financial hardship, language barrier, domestic 
violence situation):
- Take extra time and patience
- Offer to repeat information or explain in simpler terms
- For financial hardship: Offer payment arrangement (up to 3-month 
  payment plan with no interest, within your authority)
- For language barriers: Use the NovaTel Language Line (dial ext. 8888 
  for interpreter services in 150+ languages)
- For suspected domestic violence or elder abuse situations: 
  Do NOT ask probing questions. Offer to provide the customer with 
  contact information for local support services if they request it. 
  Do not make notes about suspected abuse in the main account — use 
  the confidential notes section.

SECTION 6: DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
====================================================================

6.1 EVERY INTERACTION MUST INCLUDE IN CRM:
- Date and time
- Customer name and account number
- Contact channel (phone, chat, email, in-store)
- Issue category (select from dropdown)
- Detailed description of the issue
- All troubleshooting steps attempted
- Resolution applied
- Any credits or adjustments made (amount and reason)
- Follow-up required (yes/no, with details)
- Customer sentiment (satisfied, neutral, dissatisfied)

6.2 WHEN ESCALATING, ADDITIONALLY DOCUMENT:
- Reason for escalation
- Team escalated to and transfer code used
- Whether warm or cold transfer
- Any commitments made to the customer about resolution timeline

Failure to properly document interactions is a performance issue and 
will be addressed in quality reviews.
